1. Farmacia — Start Here

The green cross. Italian pharmacists assess symptoms and recommend directly — use for fever, flu, sore throat, stomach, food poisoning, allergies, bites, eye and skin reactions.

Tachipirina — fever / pain
OKI — anti-inflammatory
Imodium — diarrhea

Many close 1–4 PM. Search farmacia aperta ora for one open now, or look for a posted Farmacia di Turno sign — one is always on rotation. Most speak basic English; use the Symptom Card tab in smaller towns.

2. Walk-In Doctor

Guardia Medica — evenings, weekends, holidays. Short wait, real doctor, ~€20–30, can prescribe. In summer coastal/resort areas look for Guardia Medica Turistica (not available in Rome, Florence, or most inland cities).

Non-urgent questions: call 116117 (works in Rome/Lazio and most regions).

3. Emergency Only — Pronto Soccorso

Call 118 or go to Pronto Soccorso. Seen by urgency, not arrival order — non-urgent waits can be long. You will be treated; as a US visitor you will receive a bill. Keep all itemized receipts for your insurer.

Bring your passport to any clinic or hospital. Keep some cash for small payments.
